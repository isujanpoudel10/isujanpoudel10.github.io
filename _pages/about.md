I am a Computer Engineering researcher at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln with interests in wireless communications, machine learning for physical-layer systems, signal processing, MIMO, beamforming, and FPGA/embedded system design. My work spans wireless channel modeling, near-field and THz communication analysis, learning-based communication systems, and practical implementation across simulation and hardware platforms.

I am currently a graduate student and researcher in Computer Engineering at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. My academic background combines communication systems, embedded design, and machine learning, and I am especially interested in building intelligent wireless systems that connect theory, simulation, and real-world implementation.

## Research Interests

- Wireless communications
- Machine learning for physical-layer systems
- Channel estimation and CSI feedback
- MIMO and beamforming
- THz and near-field communication
- Signal processing
- FPGA and embedded systems
- Semantic communications

## Skills

- Programming: Python, MATLAB, C++, C, VHDL, Linux, MySQL
- Wireless and signal processing: wireless communication systems, RAN concepts, GNU Radio, MATLAB/Simulink, electromagnetic modeling, near-field propagation, THz communication, signal processing
- Machine learning: PyTorch, Keras, GANs, autoencoders, neural networks, deep learning
- Hardware and lab: FPGA, Raspberry Pi, Arduino, oscilloscope, spectrum analyzer, signal generator, network analyzer
- Tools: Git, Wireshark, Cisco Packet Tracer, NS2, Microsoft Azure
